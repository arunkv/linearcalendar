Linear Calendar

Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 13, 2026  ·  Last updated: March 13, 2026

Short version: Linear Calendar stores all your events and tags locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to our servers. When you optionally sign in with Google, we use the Google Calendar API only to read your events for display — we never store your Google data on any server we control.

1. Who We Are

Linear Calendar (linearcalendar.app) is a free, open-source web application created and maintained by Arun K Viswanathan. The source code is available on GitHub.

2. Information We Collect

2a. Data You Create (Local Storage)

Events and tags you create in Linear Calendar are saved in your browser's localStorage. This data:

  • Never leaves your device.
  • Is not transmitted to any server operated by Linear Calendar.
  • Persists until you clear it via the "Clear" button or your browser's own data-clearing tools.
  • Can be exported at any time as a standard .ics file.

2b. Google Account Data (Optional)

If you choose to sign in with Google, we request the following OAuth scopes:

  • openid / profile / email — to display your name, email address, and profile picture in the app header.
  • Google Calendar (read-only) — to fetch your Google Calendar events and show them alongside your locally created events.

Google Calendar data is fetched directly from Google's servers into your browser session. It is not stored on any server we operate. When your session ends, this data is no longer accessible to the application unless you sign in again.

We do not request write access to your Google Calendar and will never modify, delete, or create events in your Google Calendar.

3. Cookies and Local Storage

Linear Calendar does not use advertising cookies or tracking cookies. The application may store the following items in your browser's local storage:

  • events — your calendar events.
  • tags — your tag definitions.
  • theme — your light/dark mode preference.
  • helpSeen — whether you have dismissed the welcome dialog.

Google's authentication flow may set cookies on Google-owned domains. These are governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

4. Third-Party Services

Linear Calendar integrates with the following third-party services:

  • Google Sign-In and Google Calendar API — used only when you explicitly initiate the sign-in flow. Governed by Google's Privacy Policy and Google API Services User Data Policy.

Linear Calendar's use of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

5. Analytics and Tracking

We do not use any analytics, telemetry, or tracking services. No usage data is collected.

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell, trade, rent, or otherwise share any of your data with third parties, because we do not collect or store any of your data on our servers in the first place.

7. Data Retention and Deletion

All locally stored data (events, tags, preferences) can be deleted at any time:

  • Use the Clear button in the app header to delete all events and tags.
  • Use your browser's "Clear site data" or equivalent setting to remove all local storage, including theme and help-dismissed preferences.
  • Sign out of your Google account within the app to end the Google session. Your Google Calendar data is not retained after signing out.

8. Children's Privacy

Linear Calendar is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children under 13.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the application after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.

10. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, please open an issue on our GitHub repository.
